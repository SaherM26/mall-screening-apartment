Retail leasing

Retail paths I would pitch first.

Flagship

A flagship at MOA can feel like part store, part attraction, and part launch event. The audience is already there, which makes the first impression easier.

Best for: global retail, sport, beauty, tech, lifestyle.

First Midwest store

For brands entering the Midwest, MOA gives visibility quickly. It is easier to test a new concept when the location already has a built-in audience.

Best for: digitally native brands, premium concepts, category leaders.

Pop-up test

Short-term retail can connect to holidays, creator events, product drops, and big traffic weekends without needing a permanent store first.

Best for: fandom, campaigns, holiday demand, product drops.

Food and beverage

Food is a trip driver, a social anchor, and the bridge between shopping, attractions, hotels, and event nights.

Best for: chef-led concepts, experiential QSR, premium casual.

Luxury + lifestyle

Luxury needed its own slide.

I kept luxury separate because premium brands need a different argument. They care about traffic, but they also care about context, arrival experience, and who else is nearby.

Dining + lifestyle
